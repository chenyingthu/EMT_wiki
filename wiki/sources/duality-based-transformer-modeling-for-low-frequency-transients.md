**作者**: Periodicals

## 摘要

The objective of this two-part paper is to provide clarity to physical concepts used in the field of transformer mod- eling, to dispel common misconceptions regarding numerical in- stabilities, and to present unified modeling techniques for low- frequency transients. The paper focuses on proper modeling of nonlinearities (magnetizing branches), since these components are critical to determine the low-frequency behavior. A good low- frequency model should properly represent: normal operation, inrush currents, open and short circuit, out-of-phase synchroniza- tion transient of step-up transformers, geomagnetic induced cur- rents, ferroresonance, and harmonics.
