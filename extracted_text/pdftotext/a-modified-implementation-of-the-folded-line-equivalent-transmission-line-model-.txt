Keywords:                                                   This paper proposes a modified implementation of the Folded Line Equivalent transmission line model to
Transmission line modeling                                  represent three-phase transmission lines. The Folded line equivalent decomposes a line into its open circuit and
Electromagnetic transients                                  short circuit contributions. We propose an alternative orthogonal matrix to transform single-phase transmission
Folded Line Equivalent
                                                            line parameters, voltages, and currents to the Folded Line Equivalent domain. Because the proposed matrix
EMT-type programs
                                                            is orthogonal, it can directly represent bidirectional transformations using circuit components in simulation
                                                            software, such as the ATP-EMTP. First, the circuit implementation of Clarke’s matrix decouples a three-phase
                                                            transmission line into its modes. Then, the circuit implementation of the proposed matrix decouples each
                                                            mode into its open circuit and short circuit contributions. This paper proves that the proposed approach
                                                            outputs similar results as those obtained by the Universal Line Model and the JMarti model in the simulation
                                                            of transmission lines under open-circuit, energization, and fault conditions. However, because the proposed
                                                            model does not derive from the method of characteristics, it can run with simulation times greater than the
                                                            propagation delay of the transmission line. It is shown that the proposed approach outputs accurate results for
                                                            time steps that are 10%, 100%, 200%, and 400% of the propagation delay of the transmission line. Thus, one
                                                            major advantage of the proposed approach is that it can be used to reduce runtimes in the representation of
                                                            short transmission lines in large complex networks by increasing time steps without compromising accuracy.
