    The product in frequency domain F(ω)G(ω) is equal to convolution in
the time domain f(t) ∗ g(t). The oscillations of the sinc function g(t)
causes the ripples in the output result. Imagine f(t) is a step function
which jumps from 0 to 1 at t = 0 s, then the convolution of f(t) ∗ g(t)
results in the overshoot whose magnitude is thus exactly the integral of
the (left) tail.
