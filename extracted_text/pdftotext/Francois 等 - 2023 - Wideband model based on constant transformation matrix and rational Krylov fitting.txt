Keywords:                                                   This paper analyzes the use of fitting techniques based on partial fraction expansions in the fitting of modal
Electromagnetic transients                                  transmission line functions and the assumption of constant and real transformation matrix (constant T) in the
Line constants                                              transformation of modal functions into phase domain. The focus is on the fitting of the propagation function due
Cable constants
                                                            to its complexity compared to the characteristic admittance function. It is demonstrated for the first time that
Rational Krylov approximation
Universal line model (ULM)
                                                            using a constant T can intrinsically violate the passivity of the transmission line system depending on the choice
Wideband model (WB)                                         of frequency point for assigning the constant T. Consequently, the final rational model violates passivity at
                                                            certain frequency intervals. Second contribution is the evaluation of the fitting performance with a new solution
                                                            strategy based on the recently introduced rational Krylov fitting (RKF). The case studies suggest that RKF results
                                                            in accurate and less order models compared to the vector fitting (VF) algorithm which is the de facto method in
                                                            electromagnetic transient-type models. Finally, the fitting accuracy of the legacy constant T model based on Bode
                                                            fitting is presented in the phase frame giving a clear picture of its poor fitting performance compared to modern
                                                            methods and explaining its inaccuracies in the time domain.
