Discrete-time modelling                                      model is extracted from the simulated response of the system to a probing signal. The first two techniques derive
                                                             the model directly from the analytical equations (with several simplifying assumptions). This paper compares the
                                                             accuracy of these models using the example of a SSR-vulnerable system which includes a TCSC. The presence of
                                                             several poorly damped torsional modes and the use of a TCSC to damp them make this an excellent test case for

                                                             extent of modelling detail. The results demonstrate that this approach can be both convenient and accurate for
