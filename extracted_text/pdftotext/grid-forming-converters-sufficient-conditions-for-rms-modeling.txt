Technical University of Denmark: Danmarks Tekniske Universitet, Ericavej 147, Copenhagen, Denmark




A R T I C L E I N F O                                    A B S T R A C T

Keywords:                                                Time-domain simulations are a critical tool for power system operators. Depending on the instability mechanism
Electro-magnetic transient (EMT) model                   under consideration and the system characteristics, such as the time constants of controllers, either phasor or
RMS Modeling                                             Electro-Magnetic Transient (EMT) models should be employed. On the one hand, EMT models provide a detailed-
Voltage source converters (VSCs)
                                                         modeling of the system dynamics, thus increase the reliability of stability analysis; on the other hand, using these
Low-inertia systems
                                                         models increase the computing time of simulations, slowing down the security assessment process. To decrease
                                                         computational time, system operators could resort to phasor-mode simulations for a subset of disturbances. This
                                                         paper investigates the appropriateness of phasor-approximation models on simulating events related to power
                                                         supply and balance stability when grid-forming converters are employed for providing frequency support.
                                                         Moreover, it provides sufficient conditions and bounds for the control parameters of a grid-forming converter
                                                         under which the phase-approximation modeling of the component is still valid. The control design is carried out
                                                         using a simple transfer function approach. Time-domain simulation are performed to validate the transfer
                                                         function analysis using different control settings and different line impedance characteristics.
