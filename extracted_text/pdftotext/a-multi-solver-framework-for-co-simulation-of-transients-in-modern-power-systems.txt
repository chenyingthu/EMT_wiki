                                                                                  2.1.3. BFAST subsystem
                                                                                      In this subsystem, fast dynamics occur intermittently. For example, a
                                                                                  fault in one of the EMT subsystems may affect adjacent areas for a short
                                                                                  period, and for the rest of the time this subsystem operates in steady-
                                                                                  state or with relatively slow dynamics. Therefore, it is simulated with
                                                                                  an adaptive solver known as the BFAST (see Section 2.2) that changes its
                                                                                  simulation method and solution time-step depending on whether fast
                                                                                  transients are present or not.

                                                                                  2.1.4. Dynamic phasor (DP) subsystem
                                                                                      In this subsystem, transients are slower than those observed in the
                                                                                  EMT subsystems. Disturbances applied at the EMT subsystems have low
                                                                                  dynamic impact on this region. Therefore, it is simulated with DPs using
                                                                                  a large simulation time-step for the entire duration. DP subsystem(s)
                                                                                  may be used as a buffer region between EMT and TS solvers.
