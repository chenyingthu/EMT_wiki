              The FMI standard comes in two forms: model exchange and
           co-simulation, with the latter implemented in this work. Co-
           simulation provides an interface standard for coupling
           different simulation tools with the adoption of a master-slave
           co-simulation scheme. In co-simulation, the entire system is
           decoupled into master and slave subsystems that are solved
           independently from one another, with the data exchange as
           well as the synchronization of all slave subsystems controlled
           by the master only. The slave subsystems in co-simulation are
