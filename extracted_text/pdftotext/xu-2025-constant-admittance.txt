                                                                                                              VI. CONCLUSION
                                                                                     This paper presents a constant admittance method for

                                                                                  elimination-based EMTP-type approach. The method utilizes a

                                                                                  and historical current sources. By matrix splitting and addition
                                                                                  of the output equation, a low-order equivalent nodal voltage
                                                                                  equation is derived. The following conclusions are reached:
                                                                                     1) In comparison to the classical NEM, the EM exhibits lower
                                                                                        time-complexity, fewer computational operations, a sim-

                                                                                        the time-varying admittance matrix during simulation.
                                                                                     2) The proposed method displays a certain degree of versa-
                                                                                        tility, particularly in the context of modular or unitized
                                                                                        electrical equipment, such as the PVPP and PET.
                                                                                     3) For a system comprising 50 converters, the simulation
                                                                                        efficiency is twice as high as that of the NEM and more
