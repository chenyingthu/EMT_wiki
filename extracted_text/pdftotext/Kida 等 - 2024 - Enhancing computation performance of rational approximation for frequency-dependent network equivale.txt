Keywords:                                               This work examines two strategies for enhancing the rational approximation of Frequency-Dependent Network
Complex vector fitting                                  Equivalents (FDNE) using an 8-port FDNE featuring a frequency response marked by numerous peaks and
Electromagnetic transients                              valleys. Firstly, we employ Complex Vector Fitting (CVF), an alternative to the Vector Fitting (VF). CVF
Frequency-domain realization
                                                        eliminates the constraint of complex conjugate pairs and was originally conceived for modeling baseband
Vector fitting
                                                        equivalents through scattering parameters. The implications of CVF for admittance (or impedance) matrix
Parallelization
                                                        synthesis have not yet been previously reported in specialized literature. To enhance code performance and
                                                        remove dependence on commercial software such as MATLAB® , VF and CVF were implemented in the C-
                                                        language, utilizing a low-level linear algebra package and exploiting parallelism. We evaluated performance
                                                        by varying the model order, number of ports, and frequency samples. The results confirm the feasibility of our
                                                        approach, prompting a more in-depth exploration of the potential benefits regarding FDNE realization.
