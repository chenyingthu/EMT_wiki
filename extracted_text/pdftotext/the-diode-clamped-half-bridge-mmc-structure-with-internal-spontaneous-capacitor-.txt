3. Capacitor voltage parallel-balancing mechanism

    This section ﬁrst introduces the working mechanisms of the pro-
posed MMC, then the design of damping impedances in the clamping
diode circuits are provided and the additional power losses are ana-
lyzed.


3.1. Spontaneous capacitor parallel behavior (SCPB)

    The energy circulating between phases A-B-A and phases C-B-C in
the proposed MMC are symmetrical, hence phases A-B-A can be used to
showcase the self-balancing (deﬁned as parallel-balancing in the pro-

culating paths through the adjacent SM capacitors. The auxiliary IGBT
T1 is turned on only when the ﬁrst SM in phase A is inserted. The same
situation also exists between T2 and the ﬁrst SM in phase C. Similarly,
The auxiliary IGBTs T3, T4 are turned on only when the last SM in phase
B is bypassed. Taking T1 and the ﬁrst SM in phase A as an example, as is
shown in Fig. 4, when T1 is turned on while the ﬁrst SM in phase A is
bypassed, CAu1 will be short-circuited through the DC Bus and capacitor
CAu1 will be discharged immediately. If T1 is turned oﬀ when CAu1 is
bypassed, capacitor C1 will be paralleled with CAu1 and their voltages
will be equal. This is also the case for T2 and the ﬁrst SM in phase C.
Therefore, it is necessary to make sure that T1 is turned on only when
the ﬁrst SM in phases A is inserted. Exactly when T1 is on, the auxiliary
